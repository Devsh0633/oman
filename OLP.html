The problem, in plain terms

Running training and running a training business are two different jobs

Training in Oman increasingly runs inside a compliance system, not just a training calendar. Certificates need to hold up when a client, an accrediting body, or your own head office checks them, and that check is happening more often, not less.

Most training providers in Oman are good at the first one. Instructors show up, courses get delivered, learners get certified. The second job is different: coordinating instructors across centres, keeping a clean record of who's actually certified, proving it when someone asks. That's the part that quietly eats a week every month.
